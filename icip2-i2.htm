What Will Happen If I Take Part, and What Do I Have To Do?

If you agree to participate in this study you will first be enrolled. You will then undergo a series of screening tests, including a lung function test, to determine if you are eligible for the study. The screening phase will take one month and you will need to come to the clinic 3 times during this phase.

If you successfully pass the tests you will be assigned to one of two treatment options:

-                  Study drug every 4 weeks

-                  Placebo every 4 weeks

Which treatment you receive is decided at random by a computer (purely by chance, like the tossing of a coin). Thus, you have a 50 per cent chance of receiving the study drug. Neither you nor the study doctor will know whether you are taking the study drug or the placebo.

Assessments and procedures

After successfully completing the screening phase and being assigned to one of two treatment options, you will need to come to the clinic every 4 weeks.

At each visit your doctor will:

-                  Measure your vital signs

-                  Ask you to provide a blood sample for routine health measures

-                  Use a spirometry procedure to check your lung function. This involves blowing forcefully into a tube connected to a machine called a spirometer. The spirometer measures the amount of air you can breathe out in one second and the total amount of air you can breathe out.

-                  Give you an injection under your skin with either placebo or study drug.

You will also be monitoring two things daily at home:

-                  You will also be measuring your lung function at home using the peak flow meter. The home peak flow meter will measure how fast you can blow air out of your lungs in one breath. You will be asked to perform the test twice daily--in the morning and in the evening, before taking your regularly scheduled asthma inhaler.

-                  You will be provided with an electronic diary for you to use at home. You will need to record the results from the peak flow meter in your diary twice daily--in the morning and in the evening.

After your course of treatment is complete, you will need to come to the clinic one more time--two months after you finish taking the study drug. The purpose of this visit is to continue monitoring your safety after you finish taking the study drug.

When needed--e.g., if your asthma has worsened or you have experienced an adverse event--your study doctor might ask you to attend the clinic for an unscheduled visit to check your health.

Schedule of assessments/procedures

The planned length of time you will be in the study is 61 weeks, from your first visit to the final follow-up visit, and will include at least 17 visits to the clinic and 1 telephone contact.

The study visits are divided into five visit types. These are:

-                  Enrollment/screening: Visit 1 (week -4); Visit 2 (week -3) and Visit 3 (week -1)

-                  Treatment period: Visit 4 (week 0)-Visit 16 (week 44), treatment visits occur every 4 weeks; Visit 5 - phone contact

-                  End of treatment: Visit 17 (week 48)

-                  Follow-up: Visit 18 (week 56)

What do I have to do?

You must follow all instructions given to you about the use of other medications during the study and keep a record of any other medications that you take. In addition:

-                  You must refrain from donating blood and/or plasma from the time that you sign the informed consent and for 16 weeks after the last dose of study drug.

-                  Do not take any additional asthma medications without consulting your study doctor.

If you are a woman of child-bearing potential, you must use a highly effective method of contraception from Visit 1 throughout the study and for 16 weeks after the last dose of the study drug.

If you are a sexually active male, you must use an effective method of contraception from the first dose of the drug (Visit 4) and for 16 weeks following the last dose.

If you do become pregnant or learn that your partner has become pregnant during the trial, you should contact the study doctor as soon as possible.
